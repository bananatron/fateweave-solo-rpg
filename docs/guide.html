Fateweaver’s Quest

Solo Adventure Rules Companion

What is this page?

This document tells you how to use the helper app and the core story‑telling loop of Fateweaver’s Quest. Keep it open beside the web app as a quick reference.

1. Getting Started

  1. Open the helper app and click “Begin Adventure”.
  2. Name your hero (or hit “Random Name”).
  3. Pick an Archetype (or create one), a Talent, then press “Begin Adventure”.
  4. Read the first Story Log entry—it is your opening scene.

2. Understanding the Screen

Left panel

  • Character sheet – live stats & scars.
  • Story Clock – 6 segments; fill one when the story escalates (see §4).
  • Story Log – your running narrative. Use “Undo” to erase the last line or “Clear” to wipe everything.

Center panel (tabs)

  • Map – drop or explore rooms; the latest room is blue.
  • Actions – roll Fate Dice (2d6) or resolve combat.
  • Oracle – ask Yes/No questions for guidance.
  • Details – get quick sensory prompts for descriptions.
  • NPC / Monsters / Factions / Quests – instant generators.
  • Journal – free‑text notes that also copy to the Story Log.

3. Core Mechanics

Fate Dice

Roll 2d6 and read the table in the Actions tab:

  • Any 6 = Great Success
  • Total 8‑11 = Success with complication
  • Total 4‑7 = Failure with opportunity
  • Total <4 = Disaster

Combat Roll

Click “Combat Roll”. The text explains the cost in Scars and enemy outcome.

Scars

  • Three filled circles = you fall and the adventure ends.
  • Use “+ / –” to add or heal Scars when fictionally justified.

Story Clock

  • Add one segment when you suffer a Failure or Disaster, when time clearly passes, or whenever the fiction escalates.
  • At 6 segments a Major Event fires automatically, then the clock resets.

4. The Scene Loop

  1. Frame the scene – Where are you? What is the immediate goal?
  2. Act – Move on the map, ask the Oracle, fight, talk, or use a generator.
  3. Roll – When an outcome is uncertain, roll Fate Dice or Combat Roll.
  4. Update the fiction – Type a short sentence in the Story Log (or let buttons add it).
  5. Check clocks & scars – Apply consequences, talents, or Scars.
  6. Advance the scene when any of these are true:
    • The current question is answered.
    • A clear success/failure moves the story forward.
    • You change location (new room or travel montage).
    • Real‑world time passes and the situation cools.

5. Example of Play

Set‑up: Brynn the Rogue with the “Lucky Break” talent. Goal: “Find the lost artifact of their ancestors.” Starting room drawn.

Story Log
---------
Brynn the Rogue begins their quest: Find the lost artifact of their ancestors

> Player clicks “New Room”.
You discover a new room: Monster

> Player clicks “Explore Current Room”.
Encountered: Armored Beast that collects shiny objects

> Player clicks “Combat Roll”.
4 + 3 = 7 - Failure with opportunity
You take a Scar but may retreat

(player toggles one Scar)

> Player decides to flee and clicks “Undo Last” to rewrite fiction.

4 + 3 = 7 - Failure with opportunity
Brynn dives past the beast, grabbing a glinting pendant but suffering a deep cut (1 Scar).

> Because it was a Failure, a Story Clock segment fills automatically.

> New scene: Brynn binds the wound in a safe hallway.

6. Ending the Adventure

Reach your Goal, suffer three Scars, or decide the tale is told—then write one final Story Log entry to conclude the legend of your hero.
